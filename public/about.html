Rachel Jiang

I'm a college student who dreams to make an impact in engineering research and development. I'll be obtaining a BS in Computer Science from UC Santa Barbara in June 2025, and will be starting her Masters in Electrical Computer Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University in August 2025. My research interest lies at the intersection between software and hardware with a focus on networks, security and architecture.

I'm heavily involved in the aerospace industry, as I was a 2x software engineering intern for Application Software and Flight Reliability teams at SpaceX, and I'll be returning for the Starlink Integration and Test Software team in Summer of 2025. I was also a Test and Flight Operations software engineering intern at Blue Origin. In these roles, I was worked on critical software that impact the change engineering, supply chain, manufacturing and risk assessment workflows throughout the organizations.

Since my freshmen year at UC Santa Barbara, I've been an active undergraduate learning assistant for a variety of undergraduate courses ranging from Data Structures and Algorithms, Introduction to Computer Science, Problem Solving, Formal Languages and Automata, and project-based courses. In this role, I held weekly office hours to assist students who needed help, and actively participated in teaching team decisions that would maximize the quality of student learning.

Curious and adventurous in nature, I've heavily delved into a range of projects, clubs, and research initiatives. As a controls software engineer at Gaucho Racing, I work with fellow physics, materials, electrical, and mechanical undergraduate engineers to build an electric race car for Formula SAE. My main focus is embedded software for the accumulator unit in our battery management system of our vehicle. As a research apprentice in the Security Lab, I collaborated with my peers to develop a better framework for understanding and reproducing common security vulnerabilities from the MITRE CVE dataset through using Docker containers. As an undergraduate research assistant for the Architecture Lab (ArchLab), I work with peers, graduate students, and my professor to study, design and realize a creative solution to address the growing energy and time requirements for transferring large-scale datasets within a data center.

Looking to the future, I hope to continue exploring the world of engineering through diving down the layers of abstraction within computers and electronic devices. In doing so, I'm excited to optimize the speed, efficiency, reliability and security of tomorrow's technology.
